FAQ - General

  • Pacific Pulse is a three-day indie pop, country and alternative rock festival held in Stanley Park, celebrating music, art, and coastal vibes.
  • The festival takes place from August 21–23 at Stanley Park, Vancouver, BC.
  • Gates open at 10:00 AM and close at 1:00 AM daily.
  • Yes, re-entry is permitted with a valid wristband and photo ID.

    • Tickets & Entry

  • Ticket options include General Admission (GA) and VIP passes. Group bundles and student discounts are also offered.
  • Tickets can be purchased directly through the official Pacific Pulse website here.
  • After purchase, you’ll receive an email with a digital ticket QR code for entry.
  • Yes, if the event isn’t sold out, limited gate tickets will be available.

    • Travel & Accommodation

  • Yes, limited paid parking is available near Stanley Park, you can purchase parking pass along with your ticket.
  • You can reach the festival by bus, SkyTrain, bike, or on foot. Visit the Getting Here for detailed routes.
  • Several partner hotels offer discounted festival packages. Check the Partners section for recommendations.

    • Experience & Accessibility

  • Yes, Pacific Pulse provides accessible viewing areas, pathways, and restrooms. Please see Experience for details.
  • Definitely! Many vendors offer vegan, vegetarian, and gluten-free meals. You can preview options on the Experience page.
  • Outside food and drinks are not permitted, but refillable water bottles are welcome, free water stations will be available on site.

    • Venue & Safety

  • No weapons, illegal substances, glass containers, or drones. See our Festival Rules for the complete list.
  • The festival will proceed rain or shine! Bring a poncho and waterproof shoes.
  • Yes, first aid stations are available at multiple locations, clearly marked on the Venue Map.
